This Semester: Summer Camps

Our summer camps run week by week through July and August at the Forge in Crescent City. Every camp this summer is currently full — thank you, Del Norte and Curry Counties! To ask about waitlists or get a heads-up when fall registration opens, get in touch.

Camp Sparks (K–4th) Full

A different STEAM adventure every day — science, technology, engineering, art, and math — with a take-home creation at the end of each session. Accessible, hands-on projects designed to spark curiosity in our youngest makers.

Camp Discovery (3rd–7th) Full

Bigger builds, real tools, and no easy answers. Intermediate STEAM projects with multiple solutions that push students to think creatively, solve problems, and take home something they're genuinely proud of.

Camp Tinker Bots (K–2nd) Full

Screen-free, hands-on robotics for our youngest learners. Using Sphero Indi robots and color-coded tiles, campers build mazes and courses while learning sequencing, logic, and teamwork through pure play.

Robo Rookies: Sumo Edition (2nd–5th) Full

A beginner robotics camp with a competitive twist. Campers build and program their own sumo bots with LEGO® SPIKE™ Prime, add sensors and motors, then face off in a friendly arena tournament. No experience needed.

SPIKE Lab: Engineering Edition (3rd–7th) Full

Don't just build a robot — engineer one. An intermediate camp where campers design custom gear systems and attachments to tackle open-ended, FIRST® LEGO League–style missions, working through the full design-build-test-revise cycle.

DIY Cyber Bricks (6th+) Full

Design. 3D print. Drive. A maker-forward deep dive into CAD and fabrication: campers choose a build — forklift, dump truck, ball launcher, disc shooter, bumper cars, or a combat robot — design parts, print them, and assemble a working machine to take home.

FLL Kick-Off Week (4th–8th) Full

The official launch of our FIRST® LEGO League Challenge season and the best low-pressure way to find out if competitive robotics is right for your student. Get hands-on with the new season challenge mat before anyone else. More about FLL below.

Competitive Robotics

FIRST LEGO League

FIRST® LEGO League (FLL) is our entry point into competitive robotics, for students roughly 9–14. Teams design, build, and program a LEGO robot to run missions on the season's challenge mat, and research a real-world Innovation Project alongside it. This is our third season competing, and we're the only organization within 100 miles offering competitive robotics to local youth.

The season launches each August with FLL Kick-Off Week (listed in our summer camps above) — the low-pressure way to try it before committing to a full season. Team registration returns this fall.

Competitive Robotics

FIRST Tech Challenge

FIRST® Tech Challenge (FTC) is the next step for older students ready for more. Teams design and build a full-size metal robot in CAD and in real life, code it, and compete head-to-head against teams from across the region — our Fancy Tomb Crabs (Team 25682) have carried the Forge to state competition. It's a serious engineering commitment and a genuinely transformative one.

Team registration returns this fall. If your student is curious about the jump from FLL to FTC, reach out — we're happy to talk through the pathway.
